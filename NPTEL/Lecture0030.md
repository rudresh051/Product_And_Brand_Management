## Key Tenets of Design Thinking

* **Develop a deep understanding of the consumer based on fieldwork research.**
* Using an empathic approach can be both a source of inspiration and an aid
to reaching consumer insights and discovering unarticulated user needs.
* The best way to do so is by getting out in the real world with consumers,
with open-minded collaboration, even with codesign concepts.
* Often, this involves observational research and ethnographic methods, by
watching, listening, discussing, and seeking to understand.
* The key is to start from a seeking to understand point of view and not to
persuade.
* **Collaboration, both with the users and through forming multidisciplinary teams.**
* This helps to move a company toward radical innovation, rather than
incremental improvement, and of course seeks added value.
* **To accelerate learning through visualization, hands-on experimentalism, and creating quick prototypes, which are made as simple as possible in order to get usable feedback.**
* Since design thinking is focused more on radical innovation, the more experimentation the better, and quick, simple prototypes also help grasp a potential implementation well before many resources are spent in development.
